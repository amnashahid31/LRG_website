Corporate Social Responsibility

Health and Safety Policy

LRG is committed to complying with the Health and Safety at Work etc Act 1974 and related laws to maintain a safe working environment.

KeyPoints:

• Manage health and safety across the business.
• Provide qualified resources for health and safety management.
• Equip employees with necessary training, equipment, and supervision.
• Set and monitor safety objectives through audits and performance reviews.
• Promote open discussions for continuous safety improvement.
• Aim to minimize occupational accidents and achieve a zero-accident workplace.
• Protect the health and safety of all visitors, contractors, and the public.
• Encourage employees to take responsibility for safety and report hazards.
• Foster continuous communication on safety at all levels.
• Support the reporting of accidents, incidents, and near misses.

The policy is reviewed annually and updated as needed.

Anti-Discrimination Policy

LRG is committed to promoting an inclusive and respectful work environment free from all forms of discrimination.

KeyPoints:

• We strictly prohibit discrimination based on race, color, religion, gender, sexual orientation, age, disability, national origin, or any other characteristic protected by law.
• We ensure equal opportunities in recruitment, promotion, training, and all aspects of employment.
• Any form of harassment or discriminatory behavior is not tolerated and will be addressed promptly.
• Employees are encouraged to report any discriminatory incidents without fear of retaliation.
• We provide regular training and resources to foster a culture of diversity and inclusion.
• Our policies align with relevant anti-discrimination laws to ensure compliance and promote fairness.

LRG is dedicated to maintaining a workplace where everyone feels valued, respected, and supported.

Anti-Modern Slavery Policy

Modern Slavery is a crime and gross violation of fundamental human rights.London Railway Group Ltd has a responsibility to be alert of the risks, however small, in our business and in the wider supply chain. Staff are expected to report concerns and management are expected to act upon them. This statement sets out London Railway Group Ltd actions to understand all modern slavery risks related to business and to ensure steps are maintained to prevent slavery and human trafficking.
This statement is made pursuant to section 54(1) of the Modern Slavery Act 2015 and outlines LRG’s approach to identifying and mitigating modern slavery risks.

KeyPoints:

• LRG is committed to preventing modern slavery in all business activities and supply chains.
• We provide agency staff to various industries, ensuring compliance with ethical and professional standards.
• Our supply chains consist of UK-based suppliers for materials, software, equipment, office supplies, and consultants.
• Employee training and company policies emphasize our commitment to preventing slavery and human trafficking.
• We expect our suppliers and partners to uphold the same high standards.
• Despite low risk, we periodically review our policies and procedures to ensure effectiveness.
• Any breach of laws or policies related to slavery or human trafficking is unacceptable.

Environmental Policy

LRG is committed to minimizing environmental impact in the railway and construction industries by managing waste products responsibly.

KeyPoints:

• Work efficiently to minimize environmental damage from waste generation and disposal.
• Consult with the Environment Agency or Environmental Consultants as needed.
• Set realistic improvement objectives to reduce environmental impact.
• Focus areas include compliance, waste minimization, waste management, accurate procurement, and purchasing controls.
• Protect the environment by addressing significant environmental aspects of work activities.
• Minimize the environmental impact of plant, equipment, and other assets throughout their lifecycle.
• Train and inform all staff members on environmental practices.
• Reduce waste through precise measuring and cutting.
• Reuse materials whenever possible and recycle non-reusable waste.
• Prevent pollution and continuously reduce carbon footprint.

The workforce is advised and encouraged to adhere to environmental policies and instructions to ensure compliance with the Environmental Protection Act 2021 and relevant legislation.
